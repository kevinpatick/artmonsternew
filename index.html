About the studio

What sets Art Monster apart.

Three core pillars define how the studio works: experienced artists, highly detailed work, and a wide variety of styles to match almost any idea.

  • Experienced artists
  • Highly detailed
  • Wide variety

Every artist at Art Monster is fully certified, health‑department compliant, and trained on modern equipment and sterilization standards.

Years of tattooing in St. Louis mean they’ve seen almost every skin type, placement, and style, which helps them make smart calls on design, sizing, and aftercare.

Pieces are built slowly and intentionally, from first sketch to final wipe. Line weight, contrast, and negative space are planned so the design stays readable long‑term.

Artists obsess over small decisions — from stencil placement to how shading wraps around muscle and bone — so the finished tattoo looks balanced from every angle.

The crew handles a wide range of tattoo styles: black & gray realism, neo‑traditional, watercolor, Trash Polka, geometric work, script, and fully saturated color pieces.

Whether you want a subtle fine‑line script, a bold color sleeve, or an intricate pattern build, there’s an artist who can translate the idea into a cohesive design.

FAQ

Questions before you book.

A few quick answers. If you’re unsure about anything — style, budget, or timing — mention it in your consultation request.

How do I book a consultation?

Fill out the consultation form with your idea, placement, and preferred artist. We’ll reply by email with next steps, estimated pricing, and available session times.

Do you take walk‑ins?

Art Monster is appointment‑first. When walk‑ins are available for smaller pieces, we post the details on Instagram and other social channels.

What should I bring to my appointment?

Bring any reference images, wear comfortable clothing that gives easy access to the area being tattooed, eat beforehand, and avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours.

Do you require a deposit?

Yes. A non‑refundable deposit is required to hold your date and is applied to the final cost of the tattoo. Reschedules just need reasonable notice.

Can you help with cover‑ups?

Absolutely. Attach a clear photo of the existing tattoo in your consultation form and we’ll let you know what’s realistic before scheduling.

Is the studio licensed and clean?

Yes. We follow local health regulations, use single‑use needles, and keep a controlled, low‑traffic setup so everything stays clean and focused.
